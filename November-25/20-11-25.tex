\begin{problem}
    Evaluate the following \[
    \int^{\pi/4}_0\frac{\sin^3x}{\cos^4x}\dd x
    \]
\end{problem}
Now consider the sub $u=\cos^4x$

\begin{align*}
    u&=\cos^4x \\
    \dd u &=-4\cos^3x \sin x \dd x \\
    \frac{\dd u}{-4\cos^3x \sin x}&= \dd x 
\end{align*}
And when $x=0,u=1$ and $x=\dfrac{\pi}{4},u=\dfrac{1}{4}$, thus we can transform the integral into:
\begin{align*}
    \int^{\pi/4}_0\frac{\sin^3x}{\cos^4x}\dd x &= \int^{1/4}_{1}\frac{\sin^3x}{u}\frac{1}{-4\cos^3x\sin x} \dd u \\
    &=\frac{1}{4}\int_{1/4}^{1}\frac{\sin^2x}{u^{7/4}} \dd u \\
    &=\frac{1}{4}\int_{1/4}^{1}\frac{1-\cos^2x}{u^{7/4}} \dd u \\
    &=\frac{1}{4}\int_{1/4}^{1}\frac{1-u^{1/2}}{u^{7/4}} \dd u \\ 
    &=\frac{1}{4}\int_{1/4}^{1} u^{-7/4}-u^{-5/4} \dd u \\
    &=\frac{1}{4} \Big[ -\frac{4}{3}u^{-3/4}+4u^{-1/4} \Big]^{1}_{1/4} \\
    &\approx0.19526215
\end{align*}
